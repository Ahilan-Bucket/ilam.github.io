About

I am a physics undergraduate working at the intersection of computational science, social justice, and community-focused technology.

AKF is a personal research and creative space where I document experiments, develop computational tools, and explore how scientific methods can be made useful beyond academic boundaries.

One long-term project I am developing is a flood-level simulation framework for Chennai, designed to support early warning, resource planning, and decision-making in informal settlements where conventional data sources are limited.
